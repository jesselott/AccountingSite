Managerial Accounting

Definition

Managerial accounting provides accounting information for decision makers internal to an organization. The concentration of the information disseminated contains both financial and non-financial accounts to enable managers, supervisors, foremen, and directors to make good decisions that translate into success for their organization (Miller-Nobles, Mattison, & Matsumura, 2018).

Discussion

The managerial accounting functions of planning, directing, and controlling encompass the information that business managers need to manage their plants, equipment, and human resources (Miller-Nobles, Mattison, & Matsumura, 2018).

Job Order Costing

Definition

Job order costing is the process of accumulating costs for each single unique product or specialized service. The overlying goal of job order costing is to keep track of the costs associated with the action (Miller-Nobles, Mattison, & Matsumura, 2018).

Discussion

When a job is assigned, direct and indirect costs for the job are added to the Work-In-Progress Inventory (WIP). Once the work is completed, costs are transferred to Finished Goods (FG). When the job is sold, the cost moves into Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) and is removed from FG.

Process Order Costing

Definition

An accounting system that accumulates costs by process. Used by companies that manufacture identical units through a series of uniform production steps or processes (Miller-Nobles, Mattison, & Matsumura, 2018).

Discussion

"Where process costing is discussed, it is contrasted with job costing. In job costing, the costs of relatively large and inherently dissimilar products are tracked to individual jobs. In process costing, the costs of relatively small individual products that are similar or identical are tracked to a time period, and then averaged out over the units processed" (Parkinson, 2011).

"Process costing is the appropriate approach for any situation in which homogenous units of relatively small individual value are being produced, where it is either impossible to track costs to individual products, or, though possible, tracking those costs would be prohibitively expensive" (Parkinson, 2011).

Practical Use

Practice Process Order Costing!

You have been hired in the Raw Materials Department for gourmet pizza giant Pie in the Sky. You have just received a shipment of cheese, pepperoni, and flour that are needed on the production floor ASAP. We have 500 gourmet pizzas for our dedicated staff to make, so let's get going!

Labor for crust-makers is $12/hr, for sauce specialists is $15/hr, and for our toppers, $17/hr. To process $20 worth of flour into artsy crust, it takes 1.5 man-hours.
To process $45 worth of tomatoes into diabolical sauce, it takes 2 man-hours.
To process $60 worth of pork bellies into gourmet toppings, it takes 3 man-hours.

The clean-up crew comes in nightly and refreshes the production stations for the next day's work. It takes Pie in the Sky 2 days to create 500 gourmet pizzas.


Who gets $720.00 worth of flour?


Who gets $900.00 worth of tomatoes?


Who gets $1,200.00 worth of pork bellies?
